Social-Emotional Learning with CMU HCII

At the Human-Computer Interaction Institute’s Centre for Transformational Play at CMU, I joined forces with psychologists, educators, computer scientists, and game designers from CMU, Georgia Tech, and the Pittsburgh community. Together, we explored how social-emotional learning (SEL) unfolds for youth in the playful world of Lego Brick Club.

I have been part of this project since its initiation, contributing to both its design and research execution. In the early stages, I developed a comprehensive literature database to guide the open-ended exploratory phase and designed fidelity metrics suited to the LEGO Brick Club learning environment. I also constructed multimodal datasets through interviews and on-site observations, followed by data analyses aimed at uncovering the relationships between student engagement, in-session behaviors, and social-emotional learning (SEL) outcomes. By translating these findings into actionable, evidence-based guidelines, our team enabled community organizations across Pittsburgh to facilitate playful, equitable learning experiences that cultivate sustained social growth and foundational STEM competencies in young learners.
