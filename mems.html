Thin-film Quartz Resonators

This project focused on the fabrication and characterization of thin-film quartz resonators engineered for high gain and exceptional quality factor (Q). Compared to bulk quartz resonators, thin-film quartz devices offer significant reductions in form factor and power consumption. By leveraging these properties through strategic device design, we achieved resonators with Q factors exceeding 10,000 and resonant frequencies in the 450–500 MHz range, expected to attain near parts-per-billion (ppb) frequency stability at their turnover temperature. Such tunable characteristics enable the development of high-fidelity resonators for oscillator circuits with broad applications in RF systems, precision filters, and frequency control devices.
In this project, I contributed by performing rapid characterization of the fabricated devices using a two-port vector network analyzer (VNA) across the target frequency sweep under low-pressure conditions (<200 mtorr). Based on these measurements, I selected devices for temperature-dependent studies and evaluate their turnover temperature (ToT) stability. Additionally, I participated in root cause analysis (RCA) to identify and mitigate fabrication bottlenecks, ensuring consistent device performance and yield improvement.
