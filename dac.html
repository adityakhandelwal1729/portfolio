SRAM

This project involved the fabrication and characterization of thin-film quartz resonators for high gain and Q. Thin-film quartz provides a reduction in form factor as well as low-power consumption as compared to bulk quartz resonators, wherein I contributed through rapid characterization of the fabricated devices using a 2-port VNA for various operating conditions like low pressure and elevated temperature, along with participating in RCA for fabrication bottlenecks. These resonators can be employed in Oven-Controlled Crystal Oscillators, which offer high turn-over-temperature stability, ideal for applications requiring high reliability, and in low-power oscillators with small real estate.
