Tapeout of 8-bit Segmented Digital-to-Analog Converter

I designed an 8-bit segmented high-speed Digital-to-Analog Converter (DAC), carrying out a full custom design flow from schematic creation to a Design Rule Check (DRC) and Layout Versus Schematic (LVS)-clean layout using Cadence Virtuoso in the Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC) 65-nanometer process. The DAC architecture consists of a 5-bit Least Significant Bit (LSB) binary-weighted section and a 3-bit Most Significant Bit (MSB) thermometer-coded section. The design achieves an Integral Nonlinearity (INL) and Differential Nonlinearity (DNL) of less than 0.5 across all process corners and demonstrates a Spurious-Free Dynamic Range (SFDR) of 67.6 decibels (dB) under typical process, voltage, and temperature (TTTT) conditions at a sampling frequency of 100 megahertz (MHz).
