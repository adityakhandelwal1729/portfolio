SRAM Memory: 256x16b

I designed a full-custom 256-bit single-port Static Random-Access Memory (SRAM) featuring a 64×4 partition SRAM array architecture. The design includes a 2:4 pre-decoder for partition selection, a 6:64 decoder for driving the word lines, and associated sensing and latching circuits. Each memory cell was implemented from schematic to layout using the 45-nanometer generic process design kit (gpdk) in Cadence Virtuoso.

I performed timing and Power, Performance, and Area (PPA) analysis in the typical–typical low–high (TTLH) process corner both before and after extraction. The design achieved a delay of 27 fan-out-of-four (FO4) inverter delays, an area of 12Mλ², and a power consumption of less than 2 milliwatts (<2 mW), with a unit cell area of 1460 λ².
